Your Legal Rights — Revealed

Spouse filed for divorce?

Your Rights

You have the right to contest the divorce, seek interim maintenance, retain residence in the matrimonial home, and apply for child custody simultaneously. You cannot be evicted without a court order.

Worried about child custody?

Tap to know your rights →

Your Rights

Both parents have equal legal rights to custody. Courts decide based on the child's best interest. You can seek interim custody orders, visitation schedules, and prevent the child from being taken abroad without consent.

📞 Consult Advocate Joshi

Facing domestic violence?

Tap to know your rights →

Your Rights

Under PWDVA 2005, you can obtain a Protection Order within 24–48 hours, a Residence Order to stay in your home, monetary relief, and custody of children — even before a divorce is filed.

🚨 Emergency help available
💰

Denied maintenance?

Tap to know your rights →

Your Rights

Under Section 125 CrPC, you are entitled to maintenance if you cannot support yourself. You can claim interim maintenance within weeks of filing. Non-payment can lead to the spouse's arrest.

📞 Consult Advocate Joshi
⚠️

Falsely accused in 498A?

Tap to know your rights →

Your Rights

You have the right to anticipatory bail, to present your side with documentary evidence, and to file a counter-complaint if the accusation is malicious. The Supreme Court has issued guidelines against automatic arrests.

📞 Immediate consultation advised
🏠

Thrown out of your home?

Tap to know your rights →

Your Rights

A wife cannot be evicted from the matrimonial home without a court order. You can apply for a Residence Order under PWDVA or seek an injunction to prevent being forced out of the shared household.

Frequently Asked Questions

A mutual consent divorce typically takes 6–18 months, with a mandatory 6-month cooling-off period (which courts may waive). A contested divorce can take 1–5 years depending on complexity. Advocate Joshi works to expedite proceedings while protecting your interests at every stage.

Under the Hindu Marriage Act, grounds include cruelty, adultery, desertion (2+ years), conversion to another religion, mental illness, leprosy, venereal disease, renunciation of the world, and presumption of death. Mutual consent is also a recognized ground for dissolution.

Indian courts prioritize the best interests of the child. Generally, mothers get custody of children under 5 years old. For older children, courts consider the child's preference, living situation, financial stability, and parental conduct. Joint custody is increasingly being granted. Advocate Joshi specializes in achieving favorable custody arrangements.

Courts consider the income and assets of both parties, lifestyle during marriage, duration of marriage, and the financial needs of the dependent spouse and children. There is no fixed formula — it is discretionary. Advocate Joshi negotiates aggressively to ensure fair financial outcomes for his clients.
